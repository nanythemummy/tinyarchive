General Description of Digital Archive Project-Bear Project

In this project, we plan to build a digital archive for five bear sculptures on UC Berkeley campus. Having bear as the school mascot, we can see a lot of bear related elements around the campus, including bear sculptures. They are an important part of our campus scenery and history, but there is a lack of accessible digital collection of these campus bear sculptures online. Therefore, we decide to build a digital archive for these bears, including their names, measurements (length, weight, height), color, material, installed time, location, designer, and sculptor as metadata. We also plan to include a map that marks out their locations, and hope to let tourists find them more easily.

We decide to put the sculpture California Golden Bear on our exhibit page, mainly because this is the most famous bear sculpture on campus. For each sculpture, we will add their background story as an introduction that allows the user of this archive to better know them. In order to store these sculptures in a logical way, we will demonstrate them in the order based on their installed year, from the oldest to the newest.
